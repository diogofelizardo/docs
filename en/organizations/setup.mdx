## Overview

An **Organization** is the top-level entity in Surf Data. It manages your team, projects, billing, and usage limits. Every user must belong to at least one organization.

## Creating an organization

When you first sign up for Surf Data, you'll go through the onboarding flow:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create your account">
    Sign up with your email or use a social login provider.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Complete your profile">
    Tell us about yourself:
    - **Role** — Data Engineer, Backend Developer, DevOps, etc.
    - **Company size** — 1-10, 11-50, 51-200, 201-500, 500+
    - **How did you hear about us** — Google, LinkedIn, Referral, etc.
    - **Primary use case** — Internal agents, Customer-facing, Analytics, etc.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create your organization">
    Choose a name for your organization. This is typically your company or team name.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select a plan">
    Choose a billing plan. All plans include a **7-day free trial**.

    | Plan | Price | Best for |
    |------|-------|----------|
    | Starter | R$299/month | Individual developers and small projects |
    | Team | R$549/month | Small teams with multiple datasources |
    | Growth | R$1,279/month | Growing teams with advanced needs |
    | Enterprise | Custom | Large organizations with compliance needs |
  </Step>
  <Step title="Start building">
    You're redirected to the Dashboard, ready to create your first project.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Organization settings

Access your organization settings from the **Settings** page in the sidebar.

### General settings

- **Organization name** — update your organization's display name
- **Organization ID** — your unique identifier (read-only)

### Billing

Manage your subscription, view invoices, and upgrade/downgrade your plan. See [Billing & Plans](/en/organizations/billing) for details.

### Members

Invite team members and manage roles. See [Members & Roles](/en/organizations/members) for details.

## Multi-organization support

Users can belong to multiple organizations. Use the **organization switcher** in the sidebar to switch between them.

Each organization has:
- Its own billing plan and payment method
- Separate projects and datasources
- Independent usage limits and tracking
- Its own team members and roles

<Tip>
  Use separate organizations for different clients, departments, or environments (production vs. staging).
</Tip>
